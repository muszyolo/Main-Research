### 📝 INTRODUCTION or ABSTRACT
The rising prevalence of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) has necessitated a shift from child-only interventions to holistic family support systems. Guided by the "Oxygen Mask Principle," it is critical to address caregiver psychological well-being to ensure optimal child development. Currently, support for parents is often generic, ignoring unique sociocultural and systemic barriers. 

This research bridges this gap by transitioning to "precision mental health." Utilizing the Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB), we developed the CSI-PASD instrument to measure caregivers' attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control regarding help-seeking. By combining quantitative cluster analysis with Natural Language Processing (NLP) of caregiver narratives, this study proposes a targeted, profile-informed counseling framework—supported by Digital Health Interventions (DHIs)—to mitigate caregiver burnout in Malaysia.

---

### 🎯 OBJECTIVES
1. **Instrument Development:** To develop and validate the CSI-PASD (Counseling Seeking Intention for Parents of Autistic Children) questionnaire grounded in the Theory of Planned Behavior.
2. **Data-Driven Grouping:** To segment caregivers into distinct psychological profiles (e.g., Attitude-focused, Norm-focused, Control-focused) using cluster analysis.
3. **Targeted Intervention Design:** To map structural support and digital intervention preferences (e.g., PrismChat mHealth app) to the unique emotional and systemic needs of each caregiver profile.

---

### 📊 METHODOLOGY *(Optional section if you want to add a text box in the middle blank space)*
*   **Phase 1 (Quantitative):** Deployment of the 27-item CSI-PASD questionnaire to evaluate TPB domains. Data is analyzed using K-means/Latent Profile Analysis (LPA) to identify hidden subgroups of caregiver readiness.
*   **Phase 2 (Qualitative):** NLP analysis of open-ended caregiver narratives to identify linguistic markers of distress versus the "vocabulary of resilience."
*   **Phase 3 (Validation):** Face-to-face qualitative interviews testing the feasibility of digital interventions like PrismChat among the identified clusters.

---

### 🏁 CONCLUSIONS
Treating caregiver stress with uniform, "one-size-fits-all" protocols is inadequate. By segmenting parents based on their specific psychosocial barriers and validating their lived experiences through NLP, healthcare providers and NGOs can deliver precision support. Integrating localized cultural elements (e.g., *Kampung Spirit*) with scalable Digital Health Interventions (DHIs) allows for a targeted counseling approach that effectively bridges the urban-rural mental health divide in Malaysia.
